In 2026 I’ve been living in Serra da Raiz, a small and peaceful town in Paraíba, Brazil.

Life here moves slower, and that’s exactly what makes it special. Surrounded by green landscapes, hills, and simple countryside roads, it’s a place where you can breathe, think, and reconnect with what really matters.

On many mornings, the fog quietly covers the fields, creating scenes that look like paintings. Cattle grazing calmly, birds singing, and the soft sound of the wind — everything feels natural and real. It’s the kind of place where silence is not empty, but full.

Serra da Raiz may be small, but it carries a strong sense of community. People know each other, greet each other, and share stories. There’s a warmth here that you don’t easily find in big cities.

Walking through the town, you’ll find simple houses, local markets, and that familiar feeling of home. And when the sun sets behind the hills, painting the sky in orange and purple tones, you understand why places like this stay in your heart.

It’s not about famous landmarks or tourist attractions — it’s about the feeling. The calm, the authenticity, and the beauty of everyday life.

Best place to live.
